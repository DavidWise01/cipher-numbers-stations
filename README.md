# NUMBERS STATIONS · NST

> A sphere of **UD0** — the ROOT0 universe / biosphere. Domain: **HERMES**.

NUMBERS STATIONS (NST) — Paper VI of VI, the FINALE of the AUDIO CIPHER CHANNELS series and the one channel that carries an actual cipher: a voice reads five-digit number groups over shortwave, encrypted with a ONE-TIME PAD. The carrier is the dumbest possible channel (a human voice on a $10 radio, no auth, no signature); ALL the security lives in the pad — random digits, held physically by sender and receiver, each used once then destroyed. Shannon proved in 1949 this achieves PERFECT SECRECY: the ciphertext reveals literally zero about the plaintext without the pad — not hard to break, impossible, forever (the constraint is physical, not mathematical). The lineage (WWI Morse stations → SOE/BBC coded messages → the Cold-War peak & the Lincolnshire Poacher E03 from RAF Akrotiri ~1970s-2008 → the Conet Project 1997, 150+ recordings → the courtroom: the Cuban Five & Ana Montes, 2001). Live: real mod-10 OTP encrypt/decrypt with a fresh pad — verified round-trip + the perfect-secrecy property (same plaintext, two pads → different ciphers). ⚑ The series' architectural lesson: carrier vs cipher, perfectly separated — security as a PHYSICAL key-discipline problem (never reuse it, never let it leak / hum out / be forged), not a math one. Two-layer honest: the OTP math is exact and Shannon's proof is a theorem; the demo's pad uses Math.random (not crypto-secure) and a synth voice — the key-management that makes it unbreakable is described, not enacted. Completes the six-paper set; plays as the OTP-digit layer of [[the-procession]].
